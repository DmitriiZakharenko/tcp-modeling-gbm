\subsection{Abstract}\label{abstract}

We implemented a reproducible Python pipeline for Tumor Control
Probability (TCP) modeling on the open-access CFB-GBM glioblastoma
radiotherapy cohort. The workflow covers clinical data curation,
NIfTI-based DVH feature extraction, four TCP model families (Poisson,
logistic, probit, gEUD-based), bootstrap confidence intervals, survival
analysis, and Version 3 RANO imaging endpoints with PyRadiomics
comparison.

Pooled EQD2 discriminated an exploratory overall survival (OS)
median-split proxy (ROC AUC approximately 0.68) but not RANO
non-progressive disease when dose was the sole predictor (AUC
approximately 0.43). After adjusting for fractionation scheme and
clinical covariates, pre-treatment GTV volume and author-provided
PyRadiomics features predicted early RANO with moderate cross-validated
discrimination (pooled LOOCV AUC approximately 0.64; hypofractionated
arm LOOCV approximately 0.74; PyRadiomics nested CV approximately 0.74).

\textbf{Conclusion:} classical pooled TCP dose-response validation is
not supported on routine CFB-GBM data because of limited within-protocol
dose spread and endpoint confounding. The project delivers a complete
open pipeline, formal negative result, and tumour-burden benchmarks for
future TCP feasibility studies.

\textbf{Keywords:} glioblastoma; tumor control probability; DVH; EQD2;
RANO; PyRadiomics; CFB-GBM; open data

\subsection{1. Introduction and Clinical
Background}\label{introduction-and-clinical-background}

\subsubsection{1.1 Glioblastoma and standard
radiotherapy}\label{glioblastoma-and-standard-radiotherapy}

Glioblastoma (GBM) is the most common malignant primary brain tumour in
adults. Maximal safe resection followed by radiotherapy with concurrent
temozolomide remains the standard of care for fit patients (Stupp
protocol: 60 Gy delivered in 30 fractions of 2 Gy) {[}1{]}. Despite a
uniform nominal protocol, observed outcomes vary widely because of age,
performance status, molecular factors, and tumour burden at baseline
{[}2{]}.

In elderly or frail patients, shorter hypofractionated schedules are
widely used. The CFB-GBM cohort includes 40.05 Gy in 15 fractions,
consistent with pragmatic GBM management described in Nordic and
short-course trials {[}3,15{]}. These two fractionation schemes differ
not only in total dose and fraction size but also in patient selection:
in our cohort, 60 Gy patients were younger (median age 65 vs 75 years)
and had longer OS (median 60 vs 28 weeks).

\subsubsection{1.2 Tumor Control Probability (TCP)
modeling}\label{tumor-control-probability-tcp-modeling}

TCP models estimate the probability that all clonogenic tumour cells are
sterilised after radiotherapy, as a function of delivered dose and
radiobiological parameters such as D50 (dose for 50\% control
probability) and gamma50 (steepness of the dose-response curve)
{[}4,5{]}. TCP models are used in treatment-planning research, protocol
comparison, and hypothetical dose escalation studies.

Valid TCP validation requires three conditions emphasized in the
radiotherapy modeling literature {[}7{]}:

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  \textbf{A tumour-control endpoint} (local control or a validated
  imaging surrogate), not a distant-survival proxy alone.
\item
  \textbf{Meaningful inter-patient dose heterogeneity} within the
  structure of interest (typically GTV Dmean spread greater than
  approximately 1 Gy in TCP audit workflows).
\item
  \textbf{Adequate sample size} and pre-specified cross-validation when
  predictive models are reported.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{1.3 CFB-GBM and project
aims}\label{cfb-gbm-and-project-aims}

The CFB-GBM dataset (Centre François Baclesse, n = 264 in the public TSV
release) provides pre- and post-treatment MRI, RTDOSE maps, GTV
segmentations, clinical tables, and---since Version 3---RANO response
labels and PyRadiomics feature tables {[}8--10{]}. The dataset is
distributed as pre-processed NIfTI rather than full DICOM-RT plans.

\textbf{Primary project aim (assignment Parts I--VI):} build a
reproducible TCP modeling pipeline on this open cohort, estimate D50 and
gamma50 with uncertainty, compare models, and compare our parameters
with published TCP literature.

\textbf{Secondary aim (Version 3 extension):} determine whether early
RANO imaging response and PyRadiomics features rescue dose-response
signal when classical pooled TCP fails, and report honest out-of-sample
performance (LOOCV and nested cross-validation).

Starting from 264 patients in the clinical table, 70 were excluded

58 columns (\texttt{modeling\_table.csv}), including clinical
covariates, DVH scalars, EQD2, RANO categories, and PyRadiomics merge
keys. Notebook 01 (\texttt{01\_cohort\_overview.ipynb}) documents the
full exclusion breakdown and demographic tables.

\subsubsection{2.3 Outcome definitions}\label{outcome-definitions}

\textbf{Primary TCP endpoint (exploratory):} OS greater than or equal to
cohort median (51 weeks), used as a binary proxy for pipeline testing.
This is not equivalent to formal tumour control.

\textbf{Secondary endpoint (Version 3):} RANO non-progressive disease at
t1, defined as stable disease, minor response, partial response, or
complete response versus progressive disease {[}12,13{]}. RANO non-PD
rate in the modeling subset was approximately 77\%.

\subsection{3. Data Acquisition, Curation, and
Reproducibility}\label{data-acquisition-curation-and-reproducibility}

\subsubsection{3.1 Pipeline overview}\label{pipeline-overview}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  Download clinical, treatment, RANO, and PyRadiomics TSV files
  (\texttt{python\ -m\ src.data.download\_clinical\_data}).
\item
  Merge tables and apply inclusion rules
  (\texttt{src/data/cohort\_builder.py}); export \texttt{cohort.csv}.
\item
  Download RTDOSE and GTV t0 NIfTI for 191 patients (IBM Aspera Connect;
  approximately 52 GB local storage).
\item
  Verify file completeness (\texttt{verify\_raw\_data.py}).
\item
  Compute cumulative DVH and scalar metrics
  (\texttt{feature\_builder.py}, \texttt{dvh\_calculator.py}).
\item
  Apply DVH QC (\texttt{dvh\_qc.py}); export
  \texttt{modeling\_table.csv}.
\item
  Fit TCP models, survival models, and RANO prediction suite; regenerate
  \texttt{reports/RESULTS.md} via \texttt{make\ report}.
\end{enumerate}

All analysis code lives in \texttt{src/}. Random seed 42 is fixed for
bootstrap and cross-validation splits. Notebooks 01 and 03--06 execute
top-to-bottom after \texttt{pip\ install\ -r\ requirements.txt}.

\subsubsection{3.2 EQD2 correction}\label{eqd2-correction}

To compare the two fractionation schemes on a common biologically
effective scale, we computed equivalent dose in 2 Gy fractions (EQD2)
using the linear-quadratic model with alpha/beta = 10 Gy for GTV
{[}6,11{]}:

\[\mathrm{EQD2} = D_{\mathrm{total}} \cdot \frac{d_{\mathrm{fx}} + \alpha/\beta}{2 + \alpha/\beta}\]

\subsubsection{4.2 Dose heterogeneity
audit}\label{dose-heterogeneity-audit}

A central feasibility finding is that \textbf{within-arm GTV Dmean
variation is negligible}. In the 60 Gy arm, GTV Dmean standard deviation
was only \textbf{0.28 Gy}. Therefore, classical dose-response fitting
\emph{within} a single protocol arm lacks statistical power regardless
of TCP model choice {[}7{]}. Between-arm dose differences reflect
different fractionation schedules and patient selection, not planned
dose escalation.

\subsection{10. Discussion}\label{discussion}

\subsubsection{10.1 Why pooled dose-TCP fails on
CFB-GBM}\label{why-pooled-dose-tcp-fails-on-cfb-gbm}

CFB-GBM documents routine clinical practice, not a dose-escalation
trial. Within the 60 Gy arm, every patient received essentially the
planned protocol dose (GTV Dmean SD = 0.28 Gy). TCP models
mathematically require variation in the predictor; without it, fitted
D50 and gamma50 reflect cohort mixing rather than a true dose-response
law {[}7{]}.

Using OS \textgreater= median as a TCP endpoint further confounds
interpretation: higher EQD2 patients live longer primarily because they
were younger and treated with curative-intent 60 Gy, not because
incremental GTV dose within protocol controlled tumour. When RANO at t1
is used instead, pooled EQD2 alone performs worse than random (AUC
approximately 0.43) because early imaging PD was more frequent in the 60
Gy arm despite better OS.

This negative result is scientifically valuable: it demonstrates that
\textbf{endpoint and cohort design dominate model sophistication} in
open-data TCP studies.

\subsubsection{10.2 Where prognostic signal
remains}\label{where-prognostic-signal-remains}

When fractionation scheme and clinical covariates are included,
\textbf{pre-treatment GTV volume} predicts RANO non-PD with moderate
discrimination (pooled AUC approximately 0.72; LOOCV approximately
0.64). In the hypofractionated subgroup, the effect is stronger but the
sample is small (n = 34; LOOCV AUC approximately 0.74 for volume only).

\textbf{PyRadiomics features} from the Version 3 TSV provide a modest
incremental gain over volume (nested CV AUC approximately 0.74 vs 0.70).
Optimism bias between in-sample and nested CV (delta approximately
0.04--0.07) underscores the need for pre-specified validation in future
work.

\subsubsection{10.3 Limitations}\label{limitations}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  No formal local control labels; RANO at t1 is an imaging surrogate,
  not pathological control {[}12,13{]}.
\item
  Single institution, retrospective, deceased-patients-only public
  release {[}8{]}.
\item
  Small hypofractionated RANO subset (n = 34); wide bootstrap CIs in 40
  Gy arm.
\item
  t1 GTV NIfTI masks not validated locally; t1 volumes taken from author
  TSV.
\item
  PyRadiomics features were author-provided; we did not re-extract
  locally (intentional parity with published baseline {[}9{]}).
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{10.4 Feasibility checklist for future open TCP
studies}\label{feasibility-checklist-for-future-open-tcp-studies}

\begin{enumerate}
\def\labelenumi{\arabic{enumi}.}
\tightlist
\item
  Report within-arm GTV Dmean IQR before any TCP fit {[}7{]}.
\item
  Prefer local control or validated imaging response over OS alone
  {[}12{]}.
\item
  Adjust for fractionation, age, and performance status {[}15{]}.
\item
  Report LOOCV or nested CV alongside in-sample AUC {[}18{]}.
\item
  Compare against author-provided radiomics baselines when available
  {[}8,9,17{]}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{11. Conclusion}\label{conclusion}

We delivered an open, reproducible TCP modeling pipeline on the CFB-GBM
cohort and completed all core assignment components: DVH extraction,
four TCP models, bootstrap confidence intervals, model comparison,
survival analysis, literature TCP parameter comparison, and Version 3
RANO/PyRadiomics extensions with nested cross-validation.

\textbf{Classical pooled TCP dose-response validation is not feasible}
on this routine-care dataset. With confounding addressed, \textbf{tumour
burden metrics} (GTV volume and selected PyRadiomics features) predict
early RANO with moderate out-of-sample discrimination. All figures,
tables, and numeric results regenerate from committed code via
\texttt{make\ report}.
